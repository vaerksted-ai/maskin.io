Questions, answered

The questions worth asking
before you sign up.

Will this actually improve business outcomes, or just make the team feel busier?
That's the right question — and the one most AI tools can't answer. Maskin is designed around the outcome loop: every bet has a stated goal, every shipped change is linked back to the signal that triggered it, and your team accumulates institutional memory that makes the next cycle smarter. Whether it improves outcomes depends on whether you're running it on the right workflows. That's what the 14-day trial is for.
How is this different from Claude Projects or ChatGPT Teams?
Those share prompts. Maskin shares state. The insight an agent spotted overnight is the same insight you open at 9am, linked to the bet you shaped yesterday, linked to the task your designer just picked up. A chat history is not a team memory — and a team memory is not a product process.
What happens when an agent does something wrong?
Every agent action is posted as a comment on the relevant object — visible to the whole team. Humans approve bets before they become active. Nothing deploys without a human in the loop. If something looks wrong, you close the bet and the agents stop. The audit trail stays.
Do I need engineers to run this?
No for hosted — sign up, invite the team, plug in a model key, go. Yes if you want to self-host, but it's one docker-compose file away.
What's a "bet"?
A scoped hypothesis with a goal, evidence, and a timeline. Borrowed from Shape Up. It's the unit where leadership judgment compounds — signals get surfaced, priorities get chosen, outcomes get tracked. Bets replace the backlog.
What models can I use?
Bring your own. Anthropic, OpenAI, local via Ollama — anything that speaks a standard API. Your key, your spend, your rate limits. No markup.
Where does my data live? Does it train anything?
Hosted: isolated tenants in EU or US regions. Self-host: wherever you put the container. Either way your prompts and context are never used as training data — they belong to you.
What does it cost?
14-day free trial — no credit card needed. After that: $20/seat/month (Pro), $200/workspace/month flat (Team, unlimited seats), or Enterprise with your own LLM and infrastructure. Self-host is always free under Apache 2.0.
